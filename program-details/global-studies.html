Program Overview

The world is increasingly interconnected, which is metaphorically referred to as a 'global village'. A deep understanding of these interconnections, networks, and complexities will help you navigate successful careers and global cultural and corporate experience.

This programme prepares you for this global journey through a combination of taught specialist courses and supervised independent research. We expect this higher learning diploma to appeal to applicants who want to acquire a good knowledge of international affairs and progress in a career in international organizations or companies with global operations.

With global interconnections, our postgraduate and post-experience diploma offers you several opportunities. You will learn about cultures, international markets, and the impact of globalization on our everyday lives and organizations.

Core Modules

  • International Relations and Diplomacy
  • Globalization and the Business Environment
  • International Management
  • Diversity Management in a Global Context
  • Migration in a Global Context
  • Researching in a Global World
  • Area Studies (5000-word Extended Essay)
  • Dissertation (25,000 words)

Area Studies Options

Choose from the following geographical or cultural spaces for your Area Studies specialization:

  • USA & Canada
  • Latin America
  • European Union
  • Great Britain
  • Asia
  • Africa
  • Australasia
  • Global Indigenous Issues

Career Opportunities

Graduates of this programme are prepared for various roles including:

  • Managers in international organizations
  • Administration officers in public or private sectors
  • Private consultants specializing in global affairs
  • Various career opportunities in international organizations
  • Further postgraduate education
  • Diplomatic services
  • International development agencies

Entry Requirements

An undergraduate degree (with a minimum Overall A- Grade) in any discipline and 3 years of experience OR a Master's Degree in any discipline with 1 year's experience.

Assessment

Comprises a combination of coursework, in-class tests, and a major research project (25,000-word dissertation).
