Program Overview

The Executive Diploma in Alternate Dispute Resolution (ADR) is a comprehensive one-year programme designed to equip participants with in-depth knowledge, practical skills, and a strong theoretical foundation in various methods of resolving disputes outside traditional litigation.

With an emphasis on mediation, arbitration, negotiation, and conciliation, the programme prepares students to address conflicts effectively and efficiently in diverse settings, including commercial, corporate, family, and international disputes. There is an option to gain the Higher Learning Diploma (HLD) by completing the extra Research Methods module and writing a longer thesis.

Programme Curriculum

  • Foundations of ADR
  • Comparative ADR Laws
  • Negotiation and Communication Skills
  • Mediation Principles and Practices
  • Arbitration Practices in India, Ghana, and Canada
  • Ethics and Professional Standards in ADR
  • Emerging ADR Trends and Global Perspectives
  • Research Methods (for Higher Learning Diploma Candidates)
  • Capstone Project and Practicum

Career Opportunities

Graduates of this programme are well-prepared for roles such as Mediator, Arbitrator, Negotiation Consultant, Legal Advisor specializing in ADR, and Conflict Resolution Specialist in corporate or non-profit sectors.

Graduates of the Advanced Diploma in Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) programme can pursue various career opportunities, including:

  • Judicial and court-related roles (e.g., court-annexed mediator, judicial mediation coordinator)
  • Government and public sector positions (e.g., dispute resolution officer, policy advisor)
  • Corporate and private sector roles (e.g., in-house mediator/arbitrator, conflict resolution consultant)
  • Non-profit and community opportunities (e.g., community mediation center director, restorative justice practitioner)
  • Academic and research positions (e.g., ADR researcher, professor/lecturer)

Entry Requirements

A first degree in a legal field or social sciences. Candidates with a law diploma or who have studied law modules as part of their degrees will be prioritized. Candidates with professional experience may be admitted subject to assessment and interview.

Assessment

In-class tests, Case Representation, Mentorship
