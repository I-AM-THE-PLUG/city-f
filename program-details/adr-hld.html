Program Overview

The Higher Learning Diploma in Alternate Dispute Resolution (ADR) is a comprehensive one-year programme designed to equip participants with in-depth knowledge, practical skills, and a strong theoretical foundation in various methods of resolving disputes outside traditional litigation.

With an emphasis on mediation, arbitration, negotiation, and conciliation, the programme prepares students to address conflicts effectively and efficiently in diverse settings, including commercial, corporate, family, and international disputes. This advanced programme includes a significant research component.

Programme Curriculum

  • Foundations of ADR
  • Comparative ADR Laws
  • Negotiation and Communication Skills
  • Mediation Principles and Practices
  • Arbitration Practices in India, Ghana, and Canada
  • Ethics and Professional Standards in ADR
  • Emerging ADR Trends and Global Perspectives
  • Research Methods
  • Capstone Project and Practicum
  • Research: Comparative analysis of ADR systems in India, Ghana, and Canada (20,000-word thesis)

Career Opportunities

Graduates of this programme are well-prepared for roles such as Mediator, Arbitrator, Negotiation Consultant, Legal Advisor specializing in ADR, and Conflict Resolution Specialist in corporate or non-profit sectors.

Graduates can pursue various career opportunities, including:

  • Judicial and court-related roles
  • Government and public sector positions
  • Corporate and private sector roles
  • Non-profit and community opportunities
  • Academic and research positions

Entry Requirements

A first degree in a legal field or social sciences. Candidates with a law diploma or who have studied law modules as part of their degrees will be prioritized. Candidates with professional experience may be admitted subject to assessment and interview.

Assessment

In-class tests, Case Representation, Mentorship, Research Thesis
