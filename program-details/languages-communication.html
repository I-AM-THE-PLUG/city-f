Higher Learning Diploma in Language, Culture and Society

Postgraduate level study of linguistic history, contemporary language issues, and cultural dynamics in global society.

Duration
1 Year
Format
On-campus / Virtual
Fees
$5,000-$7,000 CAD
Languages
English, French, Spanish, Arabic, Chinese

Program Overview

This postgraduate level award is for candidates who have a high proficiency in a language covered by the programme (English, French, Spanish, Arabic, Chinese Mandarin). Candidates will have a minimum of B2 in the target language or be native speakers with a degree or masters' degree in any subject.

Candidates will study the history of the target language, as well as contemporary issues that languages face in society, including minority languages and the dominance of English. The increasing use of artificial intelligence in the study and teaching of language will also be covered.

Applicants may have the opportunity to follow an option in interpreting or translation. This is an excellent opportunity to turn your existing language skills into marketable employment asset.

Core Modules

  • History of the Target Language
  • Language Diversity or Toward Monolingualism
  • Teaching Languages in a Global Context
  • Artificial Intelligence and Language Learning
  • English [Candidates with a B2 or higher can be exempted]
  • Research Methods
  • Research Project (20,000 words)

Optional Modules (Choose 2)

  • Advanced Translation
  • Advanced Interpreting
  • Globalization and the Business Environment
  • Diversity Management in a Global Context

Career Opportunities

Graduates of this programme are prepared for various roles including:

  • Banking and financial services professional
  • Language teacher and educator
  • Translator and localization specialist
  • Interpreter for international organizations
  • Tourism and cultural officer
  • Journalist and media professional
  • Cross-cultural consultant
  • Language policy analyst

Entry Requirements

Candidates will have a minimum of B2 in the target language or be native speakers with a degree or masters' degree in any subject.

Assessment

Formal exams, in-class tests, assignments, research project (20,000 words).
